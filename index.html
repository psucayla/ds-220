  • Each of these columns represent something important about the Titanic and the individuals on the ship. Passengerid is a number used to identify every person in the ship.

Survived column has 0's and 1's. 0 = did not survive, 1 = did survive.

Pclass is the level of importance for each passenger. 1 being most important, and 3 being least (possibly servants, chefs, etc.) 1 = first class, 2 = second class, 3 = third class

Name is the name of the passengers

Sex is the gender of the passenger

Age is the number of years the passenger had lived at the time of the Titanic sinking

SibSp is the number of siblings or spouses the passenger had on board

Parch is the number of parents or children the passenger had on board

Ticket is the ticket number of the passenger

Fare is how much the passenger paid to be on the ship

Cabin is where the passenger stayed on the ship

Embarked is which port the passenger board the ship on. (S = Southhampton, C = Cherbourg, Q = Queenstown)

All of these columns will be useful when making our queries for this dataset, so we will not delete any of them.

This may be because it is a known fact that women and children on the ship were allowed to get on the lifeboats (multiple little boats attached to the sides of larger ships incase of emergency) before men could, causing more of them to survive then to die.

5.) Create **3 graphs that separates all passengers by PClass (level of impportance; 1 being most important, 3 being least). Each graph should have the information of which port they embarked on and if they survived.**

To complete this query, we will create a 3 catplot graphs with the x-axis as where they embarked (S = Southhampton, C = Cherbourg, Q = Queenstown) and the y-axis as the number of people that survived or died.

In [12]:
sns.catplot(x ='Embarked', hue ='Survived', 
kind ='count', col ='Pclass', data = data)
Out[12]:
<seaborn.axisgrid.FacetGrid at 0x7ff6e0a183d0>

Based on the results of the code, we can see that the least amount of people died in first class, second class is int he middle, and the 3rd/ last class has the most amount of deaths on the Titanic ship. There is most likely some correlation to the number of deaths being people from the last class because they were most likely the working class on the ship; the people that cleaned, cooked, played music on the ship, served the first class passengers, etc. The people that worked on the ship were often looked at as unimportant and not as valuable as the first class people since they did not have as much money as them. We can also see that majority of the passengers out of all 3 ports embarked on the Southhampton port.
